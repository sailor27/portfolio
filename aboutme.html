About Sailor

How to refer to me

My name is Sailor, and my pronouns are they/them/their. I benefit when my collaborators use the correct pronouns without help from me. Here is a resource guide about gender pronouns.

My work history

I've worked plenty of service industry jobs, spent a two year period as a graduate student and English Composition instructor, and have been doing technology-related work for the last three years.

When I arrived in Portland in 2014, I was in immediate need of housing and employment. I started working at a call center supporting iOS devices, and later moved on to Apple CPU support.

That job was disempowering and paid $9.25/hr, but I discovered that I enjoyed troubleshooting issues and learning more about how Apple products work. I also found there a variety of other queer and trans people in similar situations to mine, a few of whom i still commiserate and collaborate with. From this point on, technology work is my survival strategy as well as my personal fascination.

My role at People's Food Co-op

Now, I work as the Technology Coordinator for a collectively-managed food co-op in SE Portland. Most of my Technology Coordinator role is project management, but I also install and implement systems and provide support to users. In addition, I work in and co-manage the retail store and serve as a Team Coordinator in the Personnel Coordinating Body.

Why I am learning programming

Learning to code is part of my personal long term survival strategy. I want to find work that sustains me, nourishes my desire to learn, and provides me opportunities to be treated as an equal contributing to a team.

I've always been fascinated by how the style and function of online space informs the human interactions within it. Before I left graduate school, I was writing about queer and trans people using tumblr and creating digital art installations.

I dream of improving or creating software tools that enable queer, anti-racist, feminist, and anti-capitalist action and survival. But also, I would love to be able to pay my rent and maybe even be able put up some of my friends who are houseless.
